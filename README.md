# Microwave Transmission and Antenna Radiation
Electromagnetic waves play a fundamental role in modern communication systems, particularly in the microwave frequency range where waveguides and antennas are commonly used for signal transmission and radiation. Understanding the behavior of electromagnetic waves in these structures is essential for students studying microwave engineering, antenna theory, and wireless communication systems.

This repository presents a collection of laboratory experiments focused on waveguides and antennas, providing both theoretical background and practical observations. The experiments aim to demonstrate key microwave concepts such as electromagnetic wave propagation, standing wave formation, impedance matching, and antenna radiation characteristics.

Through hands-on experimentation, students are able to analyze how microwave components operate within a waveguide system and how antennas transmit and receive electromagnetic energy. The laboratory activities documented in this repository include equipment setup, measurement procedures, data collection, and analysis of experimental results.

The goal of this repository is to serve as a comprehensive reference for waveguide and antenna laboratory work, allowing students and researchers to review experimental methods, understand microwave measurement techniques, and document their findings in a structured format.
